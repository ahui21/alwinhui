02 — About

Technology is only as good as who it serves.

When I was in high school in San Diego, I started building tools to help low-income and at-risk students get the academic support they needed. Nothing fancy — just the right technology in the right hands at the right time. That was the moment I realized this is what I wanted to spend my life doing: building things that make the playing field a little more level.

That instinct led me to cancer diagnostics, where I co-founded Proscia — a cloud-based AI platform that helps pathologists analyze cancer biopsies faster and more accurately. Then to Kubanda Cryotherapy, where we designed a breast cancer treatment system for rural women in South Africa and Uganda at less than 6% of the cost of existing options.

Today, I'm the founder and CEO of Almunakh, where I'm focused on the challenge that touches every community, every industry, and every family: how do we prepare for a climate that is no longer predictable?

The thread that ties all of it together is pretty simple — I believe the best technology should reach the people who need it most, not just the people who can afford it. That belief shapes everything I build.

03 — Currently Building

Almunakh.

"Turning climate uncertainty into business resilience — instantly."

Enterprise Software
Climate Risk · Operational Intelligence

Extreme weather doesn't send a calendar invite. Almunakh is an AI-native platform that helps the world's most weather-exposed organizations — airlines, retailers, cities, logistics operators — convert real-time climate signals into operational decisions their teams can act on immediately.

The goal is straightforward: help businesses and communities reduce their risk, protect their people, and stop being surprised by events they could have seen coming.

  1. i.

    Continuous monitoring

    Real-time climate and weather data woven into every layer of operations — supply chain, storefront, fleet, and community infrastructure.

  2. ii.

    Predictive risk modelling

    Forecasts built for your specific business — not generic weather dashboards repackaged as insight.

  3. iii.

    Decision-ready intelligence

    Clear, jargon-free, and built for the people who have to make the call — not just the team that runs the data.
